Role Designer, Programmer,
Narrative

(12 weeks)
Engine Unity

Chimken Crusade is an accessible bullet hell where players help Chimken the chicken escort a golden egg home, armed with only stubby legs and the wisdom of an old goat NPC. Players navigate through enemy encounters while managing movement — no weapons, just timing.

The game was originally conceived as a 2D Tactical RPG with an autoscrolling camera to pressure players into quick decisions. After scoping down, the project successfully pivoted into the focused bullet-hell you see here — a lesson in disciplined scope management.

Design Goals

The core goal was to build a game that felt complete and polished within a strict 12-week timeline, working as a team of two. Enemies and levels needed to teach the player new challenges progressively, and the dialogue system had to carry personality without slowing momentum.

My Contributions

  • Planned, prototyped, and shipped a ~20-minute game with a single partner in 12 weeks
  • Programmed and tuned the core autoscrolling camera mechanic
  • Implemented player movement, health, and a dialogue system from scratch
  • Designed and programmed 4 distinct enemy types
  • Built 4+ levels using Unity's tilemap system
  • Wrote all in-game dialogue and tutorial text
