Background

I am a game designer and user researcher from Bridgewater, New Jersey. I've been playing, dissecting, and tinkering with games since middle school — modding tactics RPGs, and eventually building games from scratch in Unity and Unreal Engine.

At USC I had the opportunity to work on two commercial USC Games titles — Bugnauts! and Unchained: Tale of Enra — each with large teams of developers. Working at that scale taught me how to collaborate across disciplines, communicate design intent clearly, and iterate quickly under real production constraints.

Design Interests

I am particularly drawn to strategy and tactics-based gameplay — games where every decision has weight and the best move is rarely obvious. I find the moment-to-moment decision space of turn-based tactics games endlessly interesting: the interplay between positioning, resource management, enemy behavior, and risk assessment.

My design work gravitates toward enemy, level, and systems design — crafting behaviors that are readable, learnable, and rewarding to outplay. I believe a well-designed enemy coupled with effective level design teaches the player how to beat it without ever breaking immersion.

Approach to Design

I design iteratively. Before writing code or building geometry, I reach for paper prototypes, 2D diagrams, or simple spreadsheet models. Getting a rough version in front of playtesters fast is worth more than weeks of polish on an untested idea.

Playtesting is central to my process — not as a final checkbox, but as a continuous feedback loop throughout development. My work on Unchained involved 100+ playtests, each generating specific, actionable data that the team used to improve systems, UI, and level design sprint by sprint.

I am most effective when I understand the player's mental model. That understanding — built through user research, observation, and direct feedback — is what separates a level that should work from one that actually works.

Studio Goals

I am interested in roles at studios that take gameplay systems and player experience seriously. Whether that means designing enemies for a tactics game, playtesting for a user research team, or designing the levels that teach players a new mechanic — I want to be where the game gets better one playtest at a time.
