Role: Level Designer

This page highlights the process I used to quickly prototype 3D environments and levels in Unreal Engine 5

Highlights

  • Quickly brainstormed and sketched different environments for creation in UE5

  • Used sketches to then quickly 'blockout' each environment in engine

  • Iterated on each blockout with 'blockmesh' to give a more detailed look to each environment

Design Process

My approach to level design in Unreal Engine 5 follows an iterative process that emphasizes rapid prototyping and continuous refinement:

1. Conceptualization

The process begins with quick sketches and rough layouts. These initial drawings help establish the core flow and focal points of each environment, allowing me to experiment with different ideas rapidly before committing to the 3D space.

2. Initial Blockout

Using basic geometric shapes in UE5, I create a rough 3D representation of the environment. This stage focuses on:

  • Establishing proper scale and proportions
  • Defining major pathways and sight lines
  • Testing basic player movement and navigation

3. Blockmesh Refinement

The blockout is then refined using more detailed blockmesh geometry. This phase involves:

  • Adding architectural details while maintaining simplified forms
  • Implementing preliminary lighting to test atmosphere
  • Playtesting to ensure smooth player flow through the space

4. Iteration and Polish

Each environment goes through multiple iteration cycles, where I:

  • Gather feedback and identify areas for improvement
  • Adjust layout and flow based on playtesting results
  • Refine the visual composition and spatial relationships
